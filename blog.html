Explore the Naini lake :

Naini Lake is a natural, kidney-shaped lake in Nainital, India, famous for its scenic beauty and the Naina Devi temple on its shores. The lake is central to the town's identity, named after the Goddess Sati's eyes (nayan) believed to have fallen here, making it a sacred site in Hindu mythology.
The lake is a popular tourist spot, offering boat rides and located beside the Mall Road.

    Origin:

    2. Its name comes from the Hindu myth where Sati's eye fell into the lake, creating its distinctive color.

    Development:

  2. The surrounding area was developed into a European colony by P. Barron in the mid-19th century.

    3. Tourism & Activities

    Boating:

  3. A primary attraction, with options for shikara and pedal boats available for hire.

    4. Views:

  4. The lake offers beautiful views, especially from the Mall Road, a prominent walkway along its banks.

    5. Naina Devi Temple:

  5. The sacred Naina Devi temple dedicated to Sati is situated on the lake's shore.

Explore the Golu Devta Mandir

The Golu Devta temple (also known as Chitai Temple) is a sacred Hindu temple in Uttarakhand, India, dedicated to Golu Devta, revered as the"God of Justice" andan incarnation of Lord Shiva. Located near Almora, the temple is famous for its unique tradition where devotees tie bells and white papers with their wishes to the deity, believing Golu Devta fulfills their requests. The deity is often depicted sitting on a horse, and the temple is adorned with countless bells, signifying granted wishes.
